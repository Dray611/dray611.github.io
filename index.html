My Journey From Soldier to Information Systems Professional

Thank you for visiting my first static website! This was my first project I worked on in my python class at LSU and wanted to share some more information about myself. I spent my first 18 years living and growing up in the beautiful state of Colorado. After graduating high school in 2017, I enlisted into the Army as an I.T. Specialist. I spent 3 months challenging myself both mentally and physically at basic training in South Carolina. After basic training I moved to Augusta, Georgia to study information technology for the Army. While I was in AIT (Advanced Individual Training), I gained a lot of knowledge and skills in computer networking, software troubleshooting, planning, designing, and testing computer systems. For my capstone project I had to work within a team to build a network from scratch. We had to make cables, configure our own routers and switches, and ensure the clients were able to communicate via email. After completion of AIT I moved to New Mexico to pursue a officer role in the military. I studied Business Administration at the New Mexico Military Institute (NMMI) and graduated with highest honors. I also earned my commission through the ROTC program at NMMI. After commissioning as an officer, I moved to Louisiana to further my education and military career. I’m currently studying Information Systems and Decision Sciences at Louisiana State University. Outside of school I’m a platoon leader for a signal company in the National guard. As a platoon leader I manage and mentor subordinate enlisted soldiers, supervise and execute training requirements, participate in meetings for planning operations, and develop training schedules. I love what I do because I get to lead and mentor my troops. The military has taught me a lot of valuable skills such as time management, leadership, tenacious work ethic, and discipline. After I graduate college, I plan on moving back to Colorado to start my career in the information systems field.
